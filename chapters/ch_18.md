Xi Pei stood on the bridge.

Floor pulsed under his bare feet. Warm slick residue clung from ten thousand units processed, conveyor belts at shift end. Signals settled through his nubs first. Administrative wavelengths: resource flows sharp, chain of command rigid, audit protocols ticking down.

He breathed through the chitin mask sealing his nose. Salt stung his sinuses. Humidity beaded on his neck, bio-suit pulling tight at elbows when he shifted, half-grown membrane itching like fresh solder flux.

0.3̅ dominated the center space. Her body spanned thirty feet, mantle rippling teal gradients. One eye fixed forward, the rest sealed. Delta Del coiled to her left, orange-red flares stabbing the dim light.

Bridge hummed. Data streams bloomed fractal from walls, equations twisting into status readouts. 0.3̅ extended a channel. Logistics clearance, clean vector. He accepted it. No distortion.

Her display accelerated. Pulses layered over telepathic bursts—deadline compression, accelerated schedule. Delta Del countered. Spikes jagged, crimson bursts shredding the water like shrapnel.

Six weeks to primary review. Hierarchy auditors inbound. Data yield suboptimal. Conversion plateaued. Three humans fried: neural overload, tissue cascade.

Xi Pei's heart thudded against the suit membrane. He knew the query. Flat slab. Silicon doped precise, layered tolerances, electrons forced through gates by voltage differentials. Back on the line, he'd run six stations. Knew yield rates by part number. Knew why the slab worked.

Network carried their frustration. Equations unbalanced. Variables undefined. Squids grasped computation. Not the substrate. Not the why.

Human brains wired for hierarchy—Party above, quotas below. Built devices to mirror it. Control at scale. Dead matter scaled where flesh failed.

His jaw locked. Ribs compressed. He broadcast.

Network wide. All active nodes. Humans. Cephalopods. Clean signal.

"I have information about your slab. Why humans built it. What it means."

0.3̅'s bioluminescence spiked. Teal fractured white-hot. One eye swiveled. Locked.

"What?"

Full probe slammed his nodes. Demand bandwidth.

Xi Pei held tolerances. Nubs locked steady.

"I know the line. Design philosophy. Human architecture behind it. Cost first."

Delta Del erupted. Body contracted violent, orange-red flares exploding in radial bursts, telepathic overlay crashing like a line supervisor's boot on his skull—*harsh bark from Shenzhen nights*.

"Cost? Specimen meat. No leverage. Spill or slurry."

Xi Pei's tongue pressed palate. Cold precision vectored back.

"Information nowhere else. Requires human systems *and* cephalopod hierarchy. Only one here bridges both. Perfect tolerances."

Probes flickered. Scans raked his attunement logs. Real-time review.

0.3̅ processed. Display slowed. Gradients reformed teal.

"Name it."

Grandmother voice echoed in his implant. Not his grandma. Irrelevant overlay.

"Timeline extension. Six weeks yours. Post-delivery: others preserved. Pods intact. No further implants. No suit progression. Isolation from fields. Viable."

Delta Del recoiled. Flares peaked crimson-black, tentacles lashing water currents into froth, probe intrusion hammering his nubs like defect scanners on overtime—volatile overload, shredding for weakness.

Blocked. His attunement held tolerances.

0.3̅'s eye narrowed. Display fractured query trees. Matrices spun cost-benefit fractals across the bridge walls, projections redlining deadlines while Delta Del's flares pulsed erratic, threatening to ignite the water with unchecked rage.

Bridge air thickened. Salt burned throat raw. Nubs itched overload.

Delta Del's flares dimmed fraction. "Bureaucracy exception needs yield thresholds. Exceed or default—disposal."

Xi Pei nodded once. "Will exceed. Defined inputs. Outputs mapped."

0.3̅'s eye drifted projections. Bars red.

Delta Del stabbed final spike. "No. Eat the bridge now."

Standoff locked: Delta Del's body quivering on eruption edge, flares coiling tighter with each telepathic jab demanding immediate cull while 0.3̅'s gradients held steady query loops spinning endless through administrative channels he'd already mapped cleaner than their own chaotic probes, her single eye boring into him as if recalculating every human failure prior against this one node's impossible precision, water currents churning from the tension until the entire bridge membrane throbbed in sync with the unresolved math hanging between them.
