# Outline

## Structure
Multi-POV science fiction. Each early chapter covers the same event — the intake/implantation process — from a different human's perspective. Same horror, different mind.

## Themes
- Primates as vermin: the full inversion of human exceptionalism
- Misunderstood intelligence: the squids are wrong about humans, coherently
- Ethical ambiguity without resolution: 0.3̅ is sympathetic and monstrous simultaneously
- The dystopia that doesn't know it's a dystopia

### Ch 3: "Xi Pei" (POV: Xi Pei, ~2500 words)
- BEATS:
  1. Xi Pei is already past intake — implant settled, nubs formed. He's been watching for days. He's mapped the squids' behavior the way he mapped the assembly line: inputs, outputs, failure modes.
  2. 0.3̅ interrogates other humans about the devices (iPhones, Samsungs). They all fail. A mathematician tries to explain the device mathematically — misses the point entirely. An engineer explains the circuit board — the squid can't process it, has no framework for dead assembled materials.
  3. A throwaway human gets eaten after failing — casual, nonchalant, the child. Not dramatized. It just happens. Xi Pei watches.
  4. The iPhone drifts in front of his tube. He recognizes it immediately. A specific model. He knows the exact tolerances on the chassis, the supplier for that glass, the line that assembled it. He worked on that line.
  5. 0.3̅'s attention turns to him. He doesn't wait. He reaches through the tube membrane toward the device — not aggressively, the way you'd reach for something you know how to handle. Recognition, not fear.
  6. The telepathic exchange: he can't explain the device in the squids' mathematical language. But he can SHOW it. He thinks through the entire assembly process — chassis stamping, glass lamination, board population, final test, packaging — in precise sensory detail. The squids receive this as something unexpected: not mathematical but procedural, sequential, granular. A different kind of intelligence.
  7. 0.3̅ is confused and fascinated. He attuned to bureaucratic channels, not technological ones — yet he knows the machine intimately. She doesn't understand why. Xi Pei doesn't explain. He understands, from years of being useful to people who couldn't understand what he was doing, that you show your value and then you wait.
- PLANTS:
  - The child's casual death — sets the stakes and the tone permanently (payoff: ongoing)
  - Xi Pei's procedural cognition vs. mathematical cognition — 0.3̅'s confusion (payoff: later, when she understands what the Party did to him)
  - The bureaucratic wavelength attunement — he hears the org chart (payoff: Pieter storyline, governance channels)
- EMOTIONAL ARC: watchful silence → calculated reveal → quiet power
- CRAFT NOTE: Heavy use of iPhone assembly vocabulary — chassis, Gorilla Glass lamination, haptic engine, board population, OLED panel bonding, final QC. This is the vocabulary only someone on the line knows. It lands as alien to the reader the same way squid math lands as alien — specific, technical, inaccessible without context. That's the point.
- STATUS: unwritten

- **Location:** Laboratory chamber

- **Beats:**
  1. 0.3̅ brings a jellyfish-like organism into the chamber. It's beautiful and wrong: fractal patterns, bioluminescent, moving with purpose. "This is your elevation," she says, not unkindly.
  2. Dylan tries to back away. His body responds. The creature follows. It's *fast*. Not predatory-fast, but inevitable-fast.
  3. The creature passes through his skin like his skin is fog. Enters through nose and mouth simultaneously. Dylan tries to scream. No sound comes.
  4. His nervous system catches fire. "Every neuron in his skull seemed to fire at once, then misfire twice." His heart slows to dangerous. His metabolism spikes. Heat and cold at the same time, injected directly into his spine.

  6. 0.3̅'s voice, now *inside* his head (or is it?): "The interface is settling. You'll adapt. Most do." He tries to respond. Can't. Wiring's been cut. He's paralyzed. He's terrified. He's also, in some distant part of his mind, *fascinated*.

- **Character Movement:** Dylan is changed irreversibly. The point of no return. But he doesn't experience it (yet) as loss.

- **Tone/Voice:** Prose becomes fragmented at the implantation moment. Short sentences. Sensory overload. Then crystallizes into Dylan's internal observation of his own paralysis. The wrongness is presented as pure fact, not as tragedy.

### Ch 7: Integration — Raj's POV
- **POV:** Dylan (now calling himself "Raj" internally; the name is not yet confirmed, just an awareness that his old identity is dissolving)
- **Location:** Recovery pod + observation chamber
- **Save the Cat Beat:** B Story begins (27-30%)
- **% Mark:** 12-15%
- **Emotional Arc:** Paralysis → awareness → strange comfort → confusion about comfort
- **Try-fail Cycle:** Yes-but (his nervous system does integrate successfully, but the success is unsettling because he *likes it*)
- **Beats:**
  1. Raj wakes — or becomes conscious — inside the pod again. His body is responding now. Fingers twitch. Then hands. Then he can move.
  2. The chitinous nodes at his temples are fully grown. Smooth. Hard. Integrated into his skull. They don't hurt. They feel like they've always been there, like an amputee discovering a phantom limb has become solid.
  3. He catches flashes of something in his mind — colors, geometric shapes, *meaning* riding beneath. He doesn't understand it. But he understands that he *could* understand it if he paid attention. It's like the moment before remembering a dream.
  4. 0.3̅ is waiting when he's released from the pod. "You're adapting remarkably quickly. Your neural architecture is... compatible." She says this with something like wonder. Like she's discovered his gift without knowing him.
  5. Raj looks at her — truly looks — for the first time. She's horrifying. Thirty feet of muscled softness, eyes distributed around her body, color rippling across her skin like she's made of liquid light. He should be terrified. Instead, he feels... seen. Known. Like she recognizes something in him that no one human ever did.
  6. "What am I now?" he asks. His voice is steady.
  7. "Becoming," she says. "That's the exciting part. We get to find out together."
  8. And he realizes: he doesn't want to leave. He's never felt wanted before. Not like this.

- **Plants:**
  - Raj's unusual nervous system compatibility (will be explained later as connected to his pre-scrambled state)
  - 0.3̅'s affection as something that works on Raj
  - The fragmentary nature of synesthesia (overwhelming, nearly-comprehensible)
  - Raj's need for acceptance overriding his need for freedom

- **Payoffs:** None yet; all plants for future chapters

- **Character Movement:** Raj moves from violation to acceptance. This is his fundamental arc beginning: *the person who was never at home in humanity finds home in being un-made*.

- **Tone/Voice:** This is where Dylan's wry deflection starts to crack. Shorter observations. Fewer jokes. More sensory detail. The internal monologue becomes stranger, less human. Words like "compatible" start appearing in his thoughts unbidden — *0.3̅'s* words, not his.

### Ch 9: Synesthesia Cascade
- **POV:** Irina
- **Location:** Isolation chamber (after her implantation, which has now occurred)
- **Save the Cat Beat:** Fun and Games begins (26-50%)
- **% Mark:** 17-20%
- **Emotional Arc:** Rage → sensory overload → forced adaptation → analytical breakthrough
- **Try-fail Cycle:** No-and (she tries to control the synesthesia, fails; tries to shut it down, fails; tries to understand it, partially succeeds)
- **Beats:**
  1. Irina's implantation happened off-screen between chapters. She's now in her own pod, nodes growing from her temples, and the *noise* is overwhelming.
  2. She's experiencing synesthesia. The cephalopod telepathy is being translated by her brain into human sensory experience. She hears her mother's voice saying things in mathematics. She sees geometric shapes that *taste* like copper. She feels pressure and texture and *meaning* all at once.
  3. She tries to scream. Her voice works, but the sound comes out wrong — it has bioluminescence in it, colors rippling as she screams. The crew watching her pod are not disturbed. They're fascinated.
  4. Slowly, under the overwhelming cascade, she starts *noticing*. The geometric shapes have structure. The mother's-voice-saying-mathematics has patterns. The copper taste appears when she recognizes a *number*. A ratio. A proportion.
  5. She recognizes it: the geometry of crystal lattices. The material science underlying the pod structure. The cephalopods are literally discussing the composition of *her own containment* across the telepathic network, and her brain is translating it as synesthesia.
  6. She stops fighting. Leans into it. Tries to follow the thread of meaning. And in following it, she begins to understand. Not fluently. But the pattern is emerging. The architecture of cephalopod communication is deeply, fundamentally *mathematical*.
  7. By chapter's end, she has a single coherent thought across the telepathic network without meaning to: *I understand the language of your buildings.* It comes out garbled, but 0.3̅ receives it and her bioluminescence spikes with surprise.

- **Plants:**
  - The mathematical nature of cephalopod communication (key to final resolution)
  - Irina's unique attunement to materials/structural channels (as opposed to other wavelengths)
  - The idea that humans can *send* across the network, not just receive
  - Irina's capacity for breakthrough under extreme pressure

- **Payoffs:** None yet; all future

- **Character Movement:** Irina moves from victim to decoder. By chapter's end, she's no longer just observing the cephalopods. She's *communicating* with them, however crudely.

- **Tone/Voice:** The prose during synesthesia is deliberately confusing, overlapping senses. "The color of a descending argument." "The weight of prime numbers." Short, disjointed. Then, as she starts understanding, the prose becomes more structured. Technical language emerging. Her analytical mind reasserting control.

- **Word Count Target:** 1,600-1,800 words

### Ch 12: The Midpoint — Raj's Question
- **POV:** Irina (with Dylan/Raj observation)
- **Location:** Observation chamber and laboratory
- **Save the Cat Beat:** Midpoint (50%)
- **% Mark:** 26-28%
