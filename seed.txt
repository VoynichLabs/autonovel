A science fiction universe dominated by sentient cephalopods. Earth is a protected galactic nature preserve — contact forbidden, removal of lifeforms illegal. A low-ranking cephalopod scientist, 0.3̅ + 2(#39FF14)ω, breaks that law. She abducts humans and keeps them as pets, conducting rogue research to prove they're more than vermin. Her goal: find a human who can explain human technology — specifically smartphones — which her society finds baffling. She implants her subjects with jellyfish-like bio-organisms that terraform their nervous systems, growing chitinous nodes at the temples that interface them with the cephalopod telepathic network. Some survive. Some are fried. She tracks outcomes with scientific interest.

Three POV humans: Dylan, a drug addict whose scrambled nervous system adapts in ways that surprise her; Irina, a Russian materials engineer who wants to reverse-engineer everything; Xi Pei, a Foxconn assembly worker who is, without knowing it, the answer to 0.3̅'s central research question.

Primates are vermin. Cephalopods don't build anything — all their technology is grown, biological, incomprehensible to human eyes. Their communication is simultaneous bioluminescence and telepathy, grounded in mathematics. Humans receive it as synesthesia. The conversion process — "Let's get you some tentacles" — is not cruelty. It's elevation. That's the horror.

Themes: misunderstood intelligence, the inversion of human exceptionalism, ethical ambiguity without resolution. Dystopia that doesn't know it's a dystopia. Deliciously dark.
