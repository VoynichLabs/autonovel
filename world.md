# world.md — Squid Story

---

## SETTING

The story takes place in an interstellar science fiction universe dominated by sentient cephalopods. Earth is a minor, protected planet in a galactic nature preserve — a backwater. Most of the story's action takes place in aquatic environments aboard a cephalopod vessel, where sound plays little to no role in communication.

---

## EARTH'S STATUS

Earth is located within a protected galactic nature preserve. Contact with its lifeforms is forbidden. Removing lifeforms from it is illegal. The crew operating in this story is there illegally, harvesting humans for the black market.

---

## CEPHALOPOD CIVILIZATION

### Dominance
Cephalopods are the dominant intelligent life form in this universe. They are not alone — other species exist, including some advanced humanoid civilizations — but primates, including humans, are considered among the lowest forms of life. The analogy is precise: humans are to cephalopods what mice are to humans. Vermin. Unclean. They befoul their environments. You cannot coexist with them. Most cephalopods find them aesthetically interesting and, depending on taste, delicious. That's the ceiling of their regard.

### Ethics
Cephalopods are not constrained by anything resembling human bioethics. Eating human meat is unremarkable — the moral weight of eating turkey. Keeping humans as pets is considered illogical, taboo, and illegal — not because of cruelty concerns, but because of the social stigma of attachment to vermin. The idea that humans might be intelligent is regarded as eccentric at best, delusional at worst.

### Technology — Biological, Not Mechanical
All cephalopod technology is biological. Everything is grown, not built. Their ships are living organisms. Their tools are organisms. Their suits are organisms. There is no mechanical technology in cephalopod civilization — no gears, no circuits, no metal. A cephalopod would regard a smartphone the way a human might regard a particularly elaborate termite mound: impressive for what it is, completely alien in its construction, and baffling as to purpose.

Cephalopod tools appear to humans as colorful fractal jellyfish or amoebas. They melt onto and into surfaces. They integrate. They grow. The distinction between tool and organism is not meaningful in cephalopod terms.

### Communication
Cephalopod communication operates on two simultaneous, inseparable channels:
1. **Bioluminescence** — rapid color displays across the body. Fractal patterns, gradients, pulses. Faster than humans can track without implants.
2. **Telepathy** — direct mind-to-mind transmission, grounded in a mathematical language built from geometry, trigonometry, and calculus. Cephalopods think in math.

These channels are not separate. A bioluminescent display without the telepathic layer is like tone without content. The math is the meaning; the light is the accent.

Humans perceive cephalopod communication as overwhelming synesthesia — color, sound, taste, emotion, and memory arriving simultaneously. Before implantation: incomprehensible. After implantation: fragments begin to resolve into something like meaning, filtered through the human's own memories and emotional associations. A cephalopod addressing a human may be received as a parent's voice, a grandmother's voice, an ex's voice. The cephalopod has no awareness of this translation artifact.

### Castes
- **Aquatic** — highest caste. Most intelligent. Most cultured. The ruling class of cephalopod society.
- **Amphibious** — mid caste. Handle land-based operations. Respected but not elevated.
- **Land-dwelling** — lowest caste. Regarded as effectively disabled. Marginalized.

### Appearance (human POV)
- 30–40 feet tall
- Multiple eyes set around the body — typically six or more
- When communicating with humans, squids close all but one eye. This is deliberate. A power display. Also a practical concession — it narrows their perception to something a human can engage with.
- Bioluminescent displays are incomprehensibly rapid to unimplanted humans. Post-implant, humans begin catching fragments and eventually full meaning.
- Individual squids are nearly indistinguishable to unimplanted humans. Implanted humans begin to differentiate them — by color signature, pattern rhythm, telepathic tone.

## THE BLACK MARKET

Humans are harvested from Earth and sold on the intergalactic black market as:
- **Pets** — rare, taboo, illegal. Requires a buyer with unusual tastes and enough status or cover to maintain the taboo privately.
- **Food** — common, unremarkable. Considered a delicacy among certain species.

The trade is illegal because Earth is a protected preserve, not because of any moral concern about humans.

## THE SHIP

The vessel is a living organism — a biological interstellar transport grown by cephalopod bioengineering. It is crewed by a small, mixed team of scientists conducting illegal research. The ship contains:
- A bridge where the crew operates
- A laboratory where 0.3̅ keeps her human subjects in suspended animation pods
- Terrariums for bringing subjects into a semi-natural environment
- Storage for 0.3̅'s collection of other specimens, also in suspended animation — described as an elaborate collection of dolls in boxes

---

## THE IMPLANTATION PROCESS

The jellyfish implants are 0.3̅'s primary research and conversion tool. They are biological instruments — grown, not manufactured.

**Process:**
1. A jellyfish-like organism is applied to the human's face. It passes through the pod membrane as if the barrier were fog.
2. The organism enters through the nose and integrates with the host's nervous system.
3. Chitinous nubs begin growing from the temples — the physical interface hardware for telepathic communication.
4. Additional jellyfish layers melt onto the host over time, forming a shimmering bio-membrane across the skin.
5. The implant connects the host to the background noise of cephalopod telepathic communication — rudimentary at first, expanding over time.
6. Over months, accumulated layers form a complete bio-suit: aquatic survival capability, bioluminescent communication, full integration with squid thought networks.

**Outcomes:**
- Some humans sync successfully. Their nervous systems adapt. They become functional within the network.
- Some humans are fried. The interface overwhelms the host. 0.3̅ tracks survival rates with scientific interest.

**From 0.3̅'s perspective:** This is not cruelty. This is elevation. She is converting vermin into something that can participate — however partially — in civilized existence. "Let's get you some tentacles." She means it as a gift.

## THEMES

- **Primates as vermin:** The central inversion. Everything humans use to justify their treatment of animals is now applied to them. The reader assembles this without being told.
- **Misunderstood intelligence:** Cephalopods dismiss human culture and technology as irrelevant artifacts of a lesser mind. They are wrong, but their wrongness is entirely coherent within their framework.
- **Ethical ambiguity without resolution:** 0.3̅ is the most sympathetic cephalopod in the story. She is also doing something monstrous. Both are true. The story does not resolve this.
- **The dystopia that doesn't know it's a dystopia:** From inside cephalopod culture, everything 0.3̅ is doing is eccentric but comprehensible. "Let's perfect you." The horror is in the warmth.
